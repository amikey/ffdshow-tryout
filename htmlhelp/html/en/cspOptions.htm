Advanced color space options

The advanced color space options page gives you control over the YCbCr <-> RGB conversion. The top half gives you control over the ITU-R BT.601/709, while the lower half is used for black and white cutoff, which control the contrast.
These settings are used only when ffdshow is doing YCbCr <-> RGB conversion. In other words, if you are playing mpeg video and the output color space is YV12 or YUY2, these settings won't be used.

YCbCr specification

ITU-R BT.601/709 are ITU-R standard for component digital video. They define how to convert YCbCr <-> RGB.

  • ITU-R BT.601 : almost all SDTV and DVD.
  • ITU-R BT.709 : almost all HDTV, HD-DVD and Blu-Ray. Please note some HD clips prepared for computer may use ITU-R BT.601 color space.

Contrast

Almost all the video are encoded using Y (luma) range 16-235. YCbCr(16,128,128) is normally converted to RGB(0,0,0).
Some video clips have Y below 16 which represent blacker than black. If you want to take advantage of this gradation at the expense of contrast, use full range or custom mode.

MJPEG

MJPEG and JPEG (used in grab filter) use so called PC.601 in most cases. In this case, PC.601 is used and the setting in the dialog is ignored.

Known limitations

  • The settings are not reflected on the fly when the input color space is NV12 or planar 4:2:2. You have to restart the video application in such cases.
  • ffdshow does not automatically find the matching color space except for MJPEG. You may want to try Seb's BTB&WTW level filter which automatically detect Y below 16 and over 235.
